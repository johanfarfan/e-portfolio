POLS 2100

United Nations Peace Keeping Operations

Starting to work on this assignment made discover a world of unknown things, researching on the United Nations official page -and some other sources- I learned that it's actually a very active organization, way more than I used to think. When I was trying to decide a conflict from the list of current peacekeeping operations I was interested in something large, and not related to the middle east.

So I found MONUSCO (UN Organization Stabilization Mission in the DR Congo), an operation in the Democratic Republic of Congo so complex that it started with another denomination and it changed because of the evolution of the conflict. Analyzing this conflict took me to study the story of DR Congo, and of course the previous mission that the UN had running in the area.

MONUSCO and other UN operations that I oversaw made me think about my Humanities class, I thought about the real reasons behind the background to this conflict as something that goes beyond just power and corruption, also the more humanistic side of all the happenings and triggering events. Inevitably I thought about Philosophy, I just wondered what ideas are happening in their heads, there are more than a couple of different groups involved in the conflict and my question is what motivates them to follow up with whatever ideals they have, and how the people of the DR congo can stand so much.

I also realized that in order to understand better some topics of any other subjects the more you know about the terms the better you can write about them, and sometimes I'll require some extra research so my arguments would be more accurate and my statements could be more extensive and explanatories.

PHIL 1000

Philosophy take us to a state of deep thinking, to analyze things that we usually ignore about life and it's meaning. It is very introspective, philosophers from the ancient Greece were already debating about existence, something that has been transmitted to our modern days, Who are we? What define us?

Probably our moral behavior has something to do with our identity, something curious about philosophy is that it doesn't lead to a definitive path, to an ultimate truth. The more basic que question is, the more complex it is to answer. And then what's the end of our actions? Why do we do what we do? Is happiness something that really defines the end of our actions in general? Something that I can agree with is that happiness is definitely our constant search, even without knowing it.

During this course every topic was something that made us think about certain postulation, and then we were taking another one, a different view of life and the human kind. The constant variation of positions and postulation studied on Philosophy1000 had me open to listen and analyze theories that at first time are not too convincing for me, as if I never know where I can find a bit of truth. I still have to work on my own beliefs, the study of Socrates influenced me to question everything, and to not hold arguments of something I'm not completely sure of how it is or how it works.

Now I feel like I'm constantly questioning myself. I've became a better thinker because I can dig better into information and arguments so I can look at the very origin of the idea and analyze if everything involved has cohesive logic.

I think philosophy in general makes you think so much about how we see and think about things that it actually enhance your sense of moral. Philosophers were the example, they were making theories and postulating ideas about life and humanity because they cared about everything they could perceive. Regardless if some of them were fighting in some way another currents of thinking, I believe that they thought they had a responsibility to educate the ones who surrounded them. This is, what I consider the most important thing I'm taking from this course, and something that will be part of my ideals.

No doubt philosophy is tightly related to Humanities, even some topics like Happiness and Ethics were in common for both classes. My Humanities class -one of the most personally constructive classes I had ever- Helped me a lot to assimilate most of the topics covered in Philosophy.
